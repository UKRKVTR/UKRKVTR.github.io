Agridera Fresh Market Eggplant Hybrids

Let's talk about eggplant! Eggplant, also known as aubergine, is a nutritious and versatile vegetable that is enjoyed by people all over the world. At Agridera, we recognize the importance of eggplant in global cuisine and are committed to breeding, producing, and marketing high-quality eggplant seeds.

Our team of experts invests heavily in research and development to provide solutions to eggplant growers and consumers in different countries. We focus on breeding improved cultivars that are resistant to common diseases, such as bacterial wilt and verticillium wilt, which can devastate eggplant crops.

In addition to disease resistance, we also prioritize horticultural traits that are important to both growers and consumers. Our eggplant breeding program aims to produce varieties with early maturity, different fruit sizes, firmness, glossy skin, and rich flavor. We also consider traits like resistance to environmental stressors, such as heat and drought, to ensure our eggplant varieties perform well in a variety of growing conditions.

Eggplant breeding programs, such as those conducted by Agirdera, are constantly working to improve the quality, yield, and disease resistance of eggplants. These programs focus on different segments of eggplant production, including greenhouse production, open-field production, and bush type production.

Greenhouse Tomato Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed for the Spanish and Turkish markets. For single and cluster picks, round fruit shape and resistances of TYLCV, TSWV, Nematodes and FORL (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. radicis-lycopersici). Some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

Open-field Indeterminate Tomatoes

This breeding is for Brazilian, Moroccan, and Turkish markets. The main focus is on large round shape fruits (200 grams plus); and resistances of TYLCV, TSWV, Nematodes and FORL. In the near future, F3 (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. lycopersici) will be included as basic resistance in our cultivars.

Open-field Bush Type Tomatoes

This breeding is for Mediterranean markets. We breed round and elongated shapes, standard sizes (140- 180 grams), and beef sizes (min. 180 grams up to 300 grams and more). We combine the resistances of TYLCV, TSWV and Nematodes as basic resistances in our hybrids. However, some hybrids may include more resistances, such as Cladosporium and Leveillula Taurica (Lv).

For open-field production, Agirdera's breeding programs focus on developing indeterminate eggplant varieties that are suitable for growing in open fields. These varieties are selected for their ability to withstand outdoor growing conditions, including temperature fluctuations, pests, and diseases. The goal is to develop eggplants that are high-yielding, have good shelf life, and are resistant to common field pests and diseases.
